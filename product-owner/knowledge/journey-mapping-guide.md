# Journey Mapping — Reference

## What It Is

A visualization of the end-to-end experience a user has when completing a task. Captures actions, thoughts, emotions, pain points, and opportunities.

## Two Types

| Type | Purpose | When to Use |
|------|---------|-------------|
| **As-Is** (current state) | Understand reality | Before designing anything |
| **To-Be** (future state) | Design the ideal experience | After understanding current state |

Always start with As-Is. You can't improve what you don't understand.

## Journey Map Structure

```
| Stage | Action | Thinking | Feeling | Pain Point | Opportunity |
```

### Columns Explained

- **Stage**: phase of the journey (awareness, consideration, action, retention)
- **Action**: what the user physically does
- **Thinking**: internal thoughts, questions, concerns
- **Feeling**: emotional state (use specific emotions, not "good/bad")
- **Pain Point**: friction, frustration, confusion at this stage
- **Opportunity**: how we could improve this moment

## Levels of Detail

| Level | When | Effort |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Quick sketch** (5-7 stages) | Early discovery, time-constrained | 30 min |
| **Standard map** (8-15 stages) | Feature design, sprint planning | 1-2 hours |
| **Detailed map** (15+ stages with sub-steps) | Complex workflows, UX redesign | Half day |

Start quick, add detail where pain points are highest.

## Emotional Arc

The most valuable insight is the emotional trajectory:

```
😊 ─── 😐 ─── 😤 ─── 😊
Start    Mid    Pain    Resolution
```

Look for:
- **Valleys**: where emotions are lowest (biggest opportunities)
- **Peaks**: where emotions are highest (protect these moments)
- **Cliffs**: sudden drops in emotion (something broke)

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Mapping the ideal, not reality | Interview users about what they actually do |
| Too generic ("user browses") | Add specific details ("user searches for X, scrolls past 3 results") |
| Missing emotions | Ask "how does the user feel at this point?" for every stage |
| No pain points found | You're not looking hard enough — observe real users |
| Journey in isolation | Include touchpoints with other systems, people, channels |

## Connecting Journeys to Stories

Each pain point is a potential story:
1. Identify the top 3 pain points (by severity)
2. For each, ask: "What would eliminate or reduce this pain?"
3. Write that as a story with AC
