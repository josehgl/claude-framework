# Impact Mapping — Reference

## What It Is

A strategic planning technique that connects business goals to deliverables through actors and impacts. Created by Gojko Adzic.

## The Four Levels

```
WHY? ──→ WHO? ──→ HOW? ──→ WHAT?
Goal      Actors    Impacts   Deliverables
```

### 1. Goal (WHY)
- Must be measurable and time-bound
- Not a feature or solution
- Example: "Reduce customer support tickets by 30% in Q2"

### 2. Actors (WHO)
- Who can influence the goal (positively or negatively)?
- Primary users, secondary users, stakeholders, competitors
- Be specific: "new free-tier users" not "users"

### 3. Impacts (HOW)
- What behavior change do we need from each actor?
- Format: "[Actor] should [behavior change]"
- Include negative: "What should they stop doing?"

### 4. Deliverables (WHAT)
- What could we build to create the behavior change?
- Multiple options per impact — we prioritize, not commit
- Include non-software: documentation, training, process changes

## When to Use

- Starting a new quarter or major initiative
- Prioritizing a large backlog
- Aligning team on what to build (and what NOT to)
- Evaluating whether a proposed feature actually serves the goal

## When NOT to Use

- Single, well-understood feature (overkill)
- Bug fixes or maintenance work
- When the goal isn't clear yet (do discovery first)

## Facilitation Tips

1. Start with the goal — if the team can't agree on the goal, stop here
2. Brainstorm actors broadly, then narrow to 3-5 most important
3. For each actor, generate impacts before jumping to solutions
4. Deliverables are hypotheses — mark confidence level
5. The map is a living document — update as you learn

## Prioritization with Impact Maps

After building the map, prioritize deliverables by:

| Factor | Question |
|--------|----------|
| Impact | How much does this change behavior? |
| Confidence | How sure are we this will work? |
| Effort | How much does it cost to build? |
| Risk | What happens if we're wrong? |

Kill deliverables where confidence is low and effort is high.
